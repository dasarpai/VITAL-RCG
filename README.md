## What Is VITAL?

VITAL is a continuous, correlated, cross-function organisational health monitoring framework. It monitors **22 organisational functions** across **four tiers**, combining automated system intelligence with structured human sensing — to detect the conditions that precede operational distress **30 to 60 days** before they become visible in conventional reporting.

Organisations do not collapse suddenly. They weaken through correlated drifts. VITAL is designed to see those drifts — and name them — before they become crises.

> *Organisations monitor functions. VITAL monitors the organisation as a system.*

## The Four Tiers

**Tier 1 — Core Functions (CF):** The operational engine — financial health, leadership, people and capability, delivery, compliance and risk, technology, and revenue health. When Core Functions degrade, the organisation feels it immediately.

**Tier 2 — Enabling Functions (E):** The organisational conditions that determine whether the Core can perform — culture and psychological safety, employee engagement, learning, stakeholder relationships, strategy direction, information flow, and financial sustainability.

**Tier 3 — Foundation Functions (FF):** Slower-moving assets whose degradation does not trigger immediate distress but whose absence removes the platform on which the Core and Enabling layers stand — brand and reputation, knowledge architecture, physical infrastructure, and supply chain integrity.

**Tier 4 — Resilience Functions (RF):** The organisation’s capacity to absorb and recover — innovation pipeline, ecosystem partnership health, CSR and social licence, and financial scenario resilience.

-----

## The 20 Named L3 Correlation Rules

VITAL’s correlation engine continuously tests for named cross-function cascade patterns. These are not generic correlations — each rule has a name, a trigger condition, a defined signal combination, and a response window.

Examples:

- **CASH-ATTRITION SPIRAL** — financial pressure driving talent exit, compounding cash stress
- **DELIVERY-REVENUE EROSION** — quality degradation leading to client relationship decline
- **LEADERSHIP RESPONSIVENESS FAILURE** — unresponsive leadership degrading psychological safety and engagement simultaneously
- **PLATFORM FRICTION SPIRAL** — technology friction amplifying delivery and capability degradation

*(Full rule registry in the Framework Description document.)*

-----

## Phenomenological Design

VITAL does not ask whether your organisation *has* a particular department. It asks whether the *activities* associated with each function are occurring — and what the signals from those activities reveal.

Structure is irrelevant to diagnosis. Structure matters only at the governance layer: who receives which alert, who is accountable for which response.

## Design Principles

**1. Phenomenological** — VITAL observes actual organisational conditions, not declared intentions or structural claims.

**2. Universal physiology** — Every organisation has the same organisational physiology. VITAL’s architecture is universal; Domain Calibration adjusts thresholds, question text, and existential parameters — it does not change the framework.

**3. Trust architecture** — Questions ask about the organisation, team, or work — never about individuals. Individual answers are never read by any human. Only aggregated signals are visible. No exceptions, including the CEO.

**4. “Not sure” as a signal** — A “not sure” response captures an information-flow gap. It is a meaningful data point, not a non-answer.

**5. Three-loop learning** — VITAL does not merely detect and alert. It learns. Every alert cycle, every Learning Register entry, and every Annual Recalibration makes the framework more accurate for this specific organisation.

-----

## Who VITAL Is For

VITAL is designed for any organisation that places value on sustainable, human-centred, learning-oriented ways of working — and is willing to act on early signals rather than wait for late consequences.

It has been developed with applicability across: management consulting, manufacturing, financial services, healthcare, education, logistics, public sector, and MSME contexts.
